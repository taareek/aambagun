Abstract

Tomatoes are one of the most commonly cultivated and consumed vegetable crops globally, recognized both as a popular fruit and a vital source of essential vitamins and minerals in the human diet. When plant diseases affect tomato plants, farmers face significant yield losses in both quality and quantity. Tomato leaf disease is the main issue; therefore, detecting leaf diseases early can reduce losses for farmers and improve production. In this research, we introduce a lightweight CNN model named TL-lightCNN, aimed at the automatic detection of tomato leaf diseases (TFD). The TL-lightCNN framework includes steps such as image collection, pre-processing, data aug mentation, feature extraction, and classification. After applying different pre-processing techniques, data augmentation is implemented dynamically in each batch, randomly transforming sample images in every iteration. Sub sequently, our method applies three well-established deep learning models and selects the MobileNet model to transfer the information learned from the PlantVillage image dataset to extract more robust features. Finally, We have developed a lightweight CNN model and trained it using a PlantVil lage tomato leaf dataset, which comprises images of ten classes, including one healthy class. Leveraging the strengths of data augmentation and robust extracted features, the proposed TL-LightCNN method attains an overall accuracy of 98%. These experimental results demonstrate that our proposed framework provides more effective and accurate prediction and classification of PLD based on evaluation metrics. Besides the classification task, our method is able to generate Grad-CAM images to visualize affected areas on the diseased leaf, which helps improve diagnostic clarity. Finally, the TL LightCNN model has been implemented into a web-based application using the FastAPI framework, allowing tomato cultivators to effectively predict different leaf diseases.
